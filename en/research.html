Research Overview

The last decade has seen significant advancements in the field of linguistics, where the rise of computational tools and the vast availability of language data have revolutionized traditional linguistic research. A range of applications (such as machine translation, natural language processing, speech recognition, and sentiment analysis) powered by linguistic insights have brought transformative changes to communication, information retrieval, and human-computer interaction.

The overarching aim of our research group is to bridge cutting-edge computational methodologies with classical linguistic theory. By integrating machine learning and artificial intelligence into language processing tasks, we seek to better understand linguistic structures, phenomena like ambiguity and polysemy, and the cognitive processes underpinning language use.
